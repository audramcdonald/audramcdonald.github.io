Audra McDonald is unparalleled in the breadth and versatility of her artistry as both a singer and an actress. Tony and Grammy Award Winner. McDonald also maintains a major career as a concert artist, regularly appearing on the great stages of the world and with leading international orchestras. An ardent proponent of marriage equality and an advocate for at-risk and underprivileged youth, she sits on the boards of Broadway Impact and Covenant House. was born in West Berlin, Germany. She was the daughter of American parents, Stanley McDonald, Jr., a high school principal, and Anna Kathryn, a university administrator. Her father was stationed in the U.S. Army at the time of her birth. Audra was the eldest of two daughters, and was raised in Fresno, California. Audra graduated from Theodore Roosevelt High School within the Roosevelt School of the Arts program in Fresno, California. Dan Pessano and Good Company Players, in the beginning of their junior company, helped Audra get her start in acting.
